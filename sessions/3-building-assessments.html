Session 3

Authoring Quizzes & Assessments

Quizzes, keys, and study guides from your notes

Summary

Using AI to author quizzes, answer keys, and study guides from lecture notes, plus how assignments and grading fit into a post-AI course.

  • Why author quizzes with AI -- quiz prep used to take two to three hours each; AI cuts that down.
  • Moving to in-person handwritten quizzes -- every three weeks, one page of handwritten notes, no devices, drop one, optional final.
  • Written response over multiple choice -- short-answer and code or diagram annotation, hardest to cheat on.
  • TAs grade against a generated key -- a grading key keeps results consistent across graders.
  • Prompting Claude to propose questions -- paste in five to eight lecture file names and ask for candidate questions.
  • Picking questions and an example quiz -- choose five plus an extra-credit question, then generate the quiz; bit masking example.
  • Answer key with a grading rubric -- the key explains each answer and gives partial-credit guidance.
  • Study guides students like -- topic list to focus studying, peppered with a few topics not on the quiz.
  • Print-ready PDFs -- one question per page, printed and stapled for in-person testing.
  • Time savings -- finals prep dropped from around twelve hours to about three.
  • Assignments as learning, not assessment -- the minesweeper bitwise assignment is for students to learn; quizzes do the assessing.
  • Grade weighting and the autograder -- quizzes and assignments each 50 percent; a GitHub action autograder scores homework on push.

Assessment in the AI Era

  • Take-home work is no longer a reliable signal
  • In-person, written-response quizzes move the signal back in the room
  • One handwritten page of notes, no devices
  every ~3 weeks:
    in-person quiz
    written responses
    1 page handwritten notes
    no laptops, no phones

The Quiz Pipeline

  • One prompt produces the quiz, the answer key, and the study guide
  • Each lands as markdown, then a print-ready PDF
  • Format rules live in CLAUDE.md

From Notes to Quiz

Answer Keys and Rubrics

  • The key carries a grading rubric for the TA
  • Points are fixed; partial-credit guidance is spelled out
  • Grading stays consistent across graders
