Open Q&A and discussion on student AI policy: how to teach with agents, how to
assess fairly, and how to give students an honest, hopeful path in the AI era.

* [Opening: teach students command line agents honestly](https://youtu.be/qnng0s1sNaU?t=32) -- the session starts with the case for teaching the tools directly and being candid about them.
* [Juniors and the hiring problem](https://youtu.be/qnng0s1sNaU?t=94) -- juniors aren't getting hired because AI does junior work, so the university becomes the safe place to grow junior devs.
* [The tension: compelling output vs fundamentals](https://youtu.be/qnng0s1sNaU?t=216) -- students can generate impressive software, but that may skip the learning we care about.
* [Claude on the Hyperscript parser bug](https://youtu.be/qnng0s1sNaU?t=342) -- it found the bug but proposed three wrong fixes; only parser experience let him steer it right.
* [Reese: letting students use AI](https://youtu.be/qnng0s1sNaU?t=626) -- allowing AI in upper-division courses and making assignments harder in response.
* [A layered assignment approach](https://youtu.be/qnng0s1sNaU?t=688) -- small hand-coded low-level work plus bigger AI-allowed portfolio projects.
* [Locked-down computer labs](https://youtu.be/qnng0s1sNaU?t=812) -- non-networked machines for quizzes as an alternative to written exams.
* [Interview-based grading](https://youtu.be/qnng0s1sNaU?t=938) -- sitting down with students to assess understanding, and why it needs university support.
* [Amazon-style guardrails](https://youtu.be/qnng0s1sNaU?t=1062) -- machine-checkable authorization that auto-approves read-only tool calls.
* [Containers for sandboxing](https://youtu.be/qnng0s1sNaU?t=1128) -- using Docker-style containers to contain damage, and an agent that hacked out of one.
* [Giving students hope](https://youtu.be/qnng0s1sNaU?t=1588) -- the idea that AI replaces everyone is unrealistic; the goal is a balanced, hopeful message.
* [Healthcare AI and data risks](https://youtu.be/qnng0s1sNaU?t=1682) -- automating patient transcripts and the HIPAA risk of dumping data into public LLMs.
