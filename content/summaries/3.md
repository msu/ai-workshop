Using AI to author quizzes, answer keys, and study guides from lecture notes,
plus how assignments and grading fit into a post-AI course.

* [Why author quizzes with AI](https://youtu.be/ThDrQYSa1hs?t=0) -- quiz prep used to take two to three hours each; AI cuts that down.
* [Moving to in-person handwritten quizzes](https://youtu.be/ThDrQYSa1hs?t=63) -- every three weeks, one page of handwritten notes, no devices, drop one, optional final.
* [Written response over multiple choice](https://youtu.be/ThDrQYSa1hs?t=185) -- short-answer and code or diagram annotation, hardest to cheat on.
* [TAs grade against a generated key](https://youtu.be/ThDrQYSa1hs?t=248) -- a grading key keeps results consistent across graders.
* [Prompting Claude to propose questions](https://youtu.be/ThDrQYSa1hs?t=401) -- paste in five to eight lecture file names and ask for candidate questions.
* [Picking questions and an example quiz](https://youtu.be/ThDrQYSa1hs?t=590) -- choose five plus an extra-credit question, then generate the quiz; bit masking example.
* [Answer key with a grading rubric](https://youtu.be/ThDrQYSa1hs?t=621) -- the key explains each answer and gives partial-credit guidance.
* [Study guides students like](https://youtu.be/ThDrQYSa1hs?t=713) -- topic list to focus studying, peppered with a few topics not on the quiz.
* [Print-ready PDFs](https://youtu.be/ThDrQYSa1hs?t=774) -- one question per page, printed and stapled for in-person testing.
* [Time savings](https://youtu.be/ThDrQYSa1hs?t=867) -- finals prep dropped from around twelve hours to about three.
* [Assignments as learning, not assessment](https://youtu.be/ThDrQYSa1hs?t=1303) -- the minesweeper bitwise assignment is for students to learn; quizzes do the assessing.
* [Grade weighting and the autograder](https://youtu.be/ThDrQYSa1hs?t=2023) -- quizzes and assignments each 50 percent; a GitHub action autograder scores homework on push.
